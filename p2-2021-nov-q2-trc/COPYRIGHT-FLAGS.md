## Source 2B — FLAG

- **Image:** TRC poster — "IF YOUR MOTHER WAS TORTURED, FATHER MURDERED … WOULD YOU BE
  SILENT" / "TRUTH. THE ROAD TO RECONCILIATION".
- **Cited as:** `http://www.saha.org.za/news/2014/September/gallery trc posters.htm`
  (South African History Archive, SAHA). Accessed 30 May 2021.
- **Creator / rights holder:** Produced by the Truth and Reconciliation Commission of South
  Africa (a statutory commission active 1995–2002) as part of its public-awareness campaign.
  Digitised and hosted in SAHA's TRC poster gallery.
- **Reason for flag:** The poster is an official work of a South African state commission from
  the late 1990s, firmly within copyright term. State/commission copyright is not the same as
  public domain, and SAHA presents the image for research/education without an open
  (Creative Commons) reuse licence. No verifiable public-domain or open licence was found.
  When unsure, FLAG.
- **Rights sit with:** the TRC / South African government, as digitised and held by SAHA.
- **Used by questions:** q7 (2.2.1, 2 marks), q8 (2.2.2, 4 marks), q9 (2.2.3, 2 marks),
  q13 (2.4 comparison, 4 marks) — 12 marks total. The same image is re-embedded in the
  `compare` source for q13.
